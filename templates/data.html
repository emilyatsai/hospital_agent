Dataset Information

Kidney Stone Dataset Overview

This application uses a clinical dataset containing urine analysis parameters from patients, used to predict the formation of kidney stones (calculi).

Dataset Characteristics
  • Total Samples: 100+ clinical urine analysis records
  • Features: 6 urine analysis parameters
  • Target Variable: Kidney stone presence (0 = No, 1 = Yes)
  • Data Source: Clinical laboratory measurements
  • Data Quality: Pre-processed and validated
Feature Descriptions

Urine specific gravity measures the concentration of particles in urine. Values typically range from 1.010 to 1.025. Higher values indicate more concentrated urine.

Urine pH measures acidity/alkalinity. Normal range is 4.5-8.0. More alkaline urine (higher pH) may increase kidney stone risk.

Measures solute concentration in urine (mOsm/L). Normal range: 300-900 mOsm/L. Higher values indicate concentrated urine.

Electrical conductivity of urine (mS/cm). Related to ion concentration. Normal range: 8.0-22.0 mS/cm.

Urea nitrogen concentration in urine (mg/dL). Normal range: 10-50 mg/dL. Related to protein metabolism.

Calcium concentration in urine (mg/dL). Normal range: 0.0-4.0 mg/dL. Elevated levels are a major risk factor for kidney stone formation.

Risk Factors for Kidney Stones
Key Indicators
  • High Calcium: Elevated urine calcium levels
  • Concentrated Urine: High specific gravity/osmolarity
  • Alkaline pH: Higher pH levels
  • Low Fluid Intake: Correlates with concentrated urine
